The Starting Point

Three things we know to be true

What Sotheby's already has
Preferred — a curated collector club
Museum tours with curators, studio visits, private previews worldwide. A relationship asset built over decades — and the right vehicle for what comes next.
The gap today
You know what they buy. Not what they love.
Collector profiles capture purchase history — not cultural passions. Who attends Vienna Philharmonic next month? Who will be in Paris for the opera season? That data doesn't exist yet.
What becomes possible

How It Works

The client journey

1
Collector opts in via Preferred+
A branded cultural profile — Sotheby's identity, powered by Culture Curious — is offered to Preferred members. They input favourite performers, composers, venues, cities. Feels like a luxury benefit, not a survey.
Sotheby's branded · White-label
2
App surfaces their cultural season — across all cities
Culture Curious syncs the global calendar and shows each collector their relevant events: opera in Vienna, ballet in Paris, piano recital in Amsterdam — all in one place. A collector going to three cities in one week now sees a coherent programme.
Value to the collector
3
Sotheby's dashboard sees the cultural map
The Preferred team gets a live view: which collectors are going where, when, and what they care about. Cultural preference enriches every existing CRM record — silently, automatically, elegantly.
CRM enrichment
4
Sotheby's activates around the moment
A collector attends a Tacita Dean–designed production at ROH → Sotheby's places a private studio visit or collector dinner the same week. No cold outreach. The timing is already perfect.
Intelligent activation
5
The relationship deepens — naturally
The collector experiences Sotheby's as deeply attuned to their world. Loyalty strengthens. Their +1s become new contacts in the Preferred pipeline. The collection — and the relationship — grows together.
Loyalty · New introductions
