Addressing Scepticism

The questions you should be asking

From doctors, nutritionists, and clients. Answered directly — no deflection.

Can AI be trusted with health decisions?
AI is the interpreter, not the data source. Facts come from deterministic code: SNP parser, ClinVar API, CPIC. Every conclusion carries a Grade A/B tag and a direct citation. You can audit every claim.
Doesn't AI hallucinate?
Zero-hallucination policy. Any claim without a Grade A/B source is either excluded or labelled "unverified." The system goes silent rather than speculate. No wellness noise.
Does this replace the GP?
No — it prepares the patient for the GP. Instead of spending 5 of 7 minutes explaining context, the patient arrives with a one-page brief: 18 months of trends, 3 key flags, one specific question. The quality of the clinical conversation multiplies.
How reliable is 23andMe data for clinical use?
For nutrition and lifestyle optimisation: yes. For clinical decisions: always flagged as "requires WGS confirmation." 553,811 SNPs, 99.1% call rate, GENOTYPED variants only — not imputed. Informational by default.
What about data privacy?
All data is processed locally on your device. The genomic file never leaves your machine. No third-party servers. No cloud storage of health data. Full control remains with the individual.
Does it work for women?
Yes. The Portfolio Diet −17% LDL result is validated on a mixed-gender cohort. A dedicated Women's Health module covers hormonal context (perimenopause, fertility, IVF) via a separate agent. Grade A
