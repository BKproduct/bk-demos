4 Biases That Cause Overpricing
01
Favorite-Longshot Bias
Traders systematically overpay for low-probability events. Contracts under 15% return 43 cents per dollar — worse than slot machines (93¢). Source: NBER WP #15923, Kalshi 292M trades.
02
YES-Asymmetry / "Optimism Tax"
Takers systematically prefer "Yes" bets on longshot events. Makers (sellers) collect this structural tax. Source: Becker 2025, arXiv.
03
Wishful Thinking + Narrative Fallacy
Geopolitical markets: traders pay a premium to see the outcome they want — regime change, revolution, ceasefire. Price becomes a proxy for desire, not probability.
04
Availability Bias (Contagion)
Post-Wagner Mutiny (2023), post-Iran strike (Feb 2026): dramatic events inflate perceived probability for 2–3 months. Market holds elevated prices long after the signal fades.
